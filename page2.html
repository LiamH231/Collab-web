About Nintendo

Nintendo was founded in 1889 by Fusajiro Yamauchi as a playing card company, and the company has evolved influential and recognizable names in the video game industry. The company initially produced handmade Hanafuda cards, and overtime the business transitioned into various ventures, including a taxi service and a love hotel. In the 1970s, Nintendo fully embraced the gaming world, and started revelutionizing the gaming world. Nintendo relased the Ninendo Entertainment System (NES) in the mid 1980s, helping to revitalize the market after the video game crash. The company is renowned for it's iconic franchises, icluding Mario, The Legend of Zelda, and Pokemon, which have become cultural Phenomena and generated billions in revenue. Known for it's commitment to innovation, Nintendo has consistently pushed the boundries of gaming technology, from the introduction of the Wii's motion controls to the hybrid design of the Nintendo Switch. With a legacy that spans over a centuy, Nintendo continues to shape the landscape of interactive entertainment through it's commitment to quality and imaginative storytelling.
